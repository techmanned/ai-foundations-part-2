What RAG Means

Retrieval-Augmented Generation means answering a question by first retrieving useful source text. This demo uses local notes, simple vectors, and a small extractive answer.

  1. Documents are split into chunks.
  2. Chunks are converted into vectors.
  3. A user question is converted into a vector.
  4. The app finds the most similar chunks.
  5. The app creates a simple answer using the retrieved chunks.

How the Vector Search Works

Chunking

Long notes are split into smaller pieces of about 500 characters. Smaller chunks make retrieval easier to inspect.

TF-IDF

TF-IDF gives more weight to words that are important in one chunk but not common across every chunk.

Vectors

Each chunk becomes a list of numbers. The question is converted into the same vector space.

Cosine Similarity

Cosine similarity compares vector direction. Higher scores mean the question and chunk use more related words.

Retrieval

The app returns the top three matching chunks and shows their similarity scores.

Simplified RAG

A real RAG system often sends retrieved chunks to an LLM. This app stays fully local and combines retrieved text into a simple extractive answer.
